What's a good Sharpe ratio for a momentum signal?
Copilot

For daily-bar momentum strategies on liquid equities, an out-of-sample Sharpe between 0.8 and 1.5 is realistic. Your AAPL project at 1.24 is in healthy territory. Anything above 2.0 on vanilla momentum should raise suspicion about lookahead bias or overfitting.

Why span=20 and not 50?
Copilot
search_knowledge 5 chunks · 0.3s
Span=20 roughly matches a monthly lookback on daily data — the standard "medium-term momentum" horizon that's worked historically on US equities. Span=50 is slower, better if you want to reduce turnover at the cost of signal speed. You could A/B them with two EMA blocks.
